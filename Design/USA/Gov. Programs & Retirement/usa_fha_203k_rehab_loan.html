203(k) Process Timeline ~60–90 Days
1
Find Property & 203(k) Lender
Not all FHA lenders offer 203(k). Use HUD's lender search at hud.gov. Identify needed repairs before making offer.
⏱ Days 1–7
2
HUD Consultant Inspection (Standard only)
Licensed HUD 203(k) consultant inspects property, creates Work Write-Up and cost estimate. Fee: $400–$1,000.
⏱ Days 7–14
3
Get Contractor Bids & Appraisal
Obtain bids from licensed contractors. FHA appraisal based on After Improved Value (AIV). Bids + plans submitted to lender.
⏱ Days 14–30
4
Loan Underwriting & Approval
Lender reviews all docs, appraisal, contractor bids. Standard 203(k) may take 45–60 days; Limited typically 30–45 days.
⏱ Days 30–60
5
Close & Renovation Begins
Funds held in renovation escrow account. Contractor draws released after HUD inspection milestones. Work must be completed within 6 months.
⏱ Days 60–90 → Closing
Key Rules & Requirements HUD 2025
🏦
FHA Loan Limits · 2025
Standard limit: $524,225 (most US counties). High-cost areas: up to $1,209,750. Includes purchase + renovation. Property must be 1–4 unit owner-occupied.
💳
Credit & Income Requirements
Minimum 580 FICO for 3.5% down. 500–579 requires 10% down. Debt-to-income limit: 31% front / 43% back. 2 years employment history required.
🔒
What's NOT Eligible
Luxury items (pools, hot tubs, tennis courts), new construction, work started before closing, repairs not approved by HUD consultant, or work done by the borrower themselves.
📋
MIP — Mortgage Insurance Premium
Upfront MIP: 1.75% of loan (rolled in). Annual MIP: 0.55%/yr ÷ 12 for loans with ≥10% down with 11-year cancellation, or life-of-loan for <10% down as of 2024 rules.
