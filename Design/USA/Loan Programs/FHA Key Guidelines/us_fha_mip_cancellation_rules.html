Path to Removing MIP
1️⃣
Build 20% Equity
Through paydown or appreciation — needed for conventional refinance with no PMI.
2️⃣
Get a New Appraisal
Lender orders appraisal to confirm current value supports 80% LTV or better.
3️⃣
Refinance to Conventional Loan
Replace your FHA loan with a conventional mortgage — eliminates MIP entirely if 20%+ equity.
⚠️
Or: Sell the Home
MIP obligation ends automatically when the loan is paid off through sale.

Key Facts to Know
📅
Rule Changed June 3, 2013
Loans before this date may follow older 78% LTV cancellation rules
🔄
Refinancing Resets MIP
A new FHA refinance restarts MIP terms under current rules
💰
UFMIP Refund on Refi
Partial UFMIP refund possible if refinancing FHA-to-FHA within 3 years
🏠
Conventional PMI is Different
Conventional loans auto-cancel PMI at 78% LTV by federal law (HPA)
