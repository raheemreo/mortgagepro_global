⚠️ Two-Close Rate Risk
If mortgage rates rise 0.50% between your construction closing and your permanent closing, that's roughly $167/mo more — about $60,000 in added interest over 30 years. One-close locks your permanent rate upfront, removing this risk entirely.
Side-by-Side Breakdown
Recommended for most
🔂 One-Time Close
✅
Single loan covers land, construction, and the permanent mortgage
✅
One closing = one set of closing costs and fees
✅
Permanent rate is locked before construction begins
✅
No re-qualification needed after the build is done
⚠️
Fewer lenders offer it; builder must be lender-approved upfront
⚠️
Harder to change scope, budget, or builder mid-project
Two-Time Close
🔁 Two-Time Close
✅
More flexibility to shop the permanent mortgage separately
✅
Easier to change plans, budget or builder mid-build
✅
Wider lender pool for the construction-only phase
⚠️
Two full closings = two full sets of closing costs
⚠️
Permanent rate isn't locked — exposed to rate moves during build
⚠️
Must re-qualify (income, credit, appraisal) at the second closing
Which Should You Choose?
🔂
Choose One-Close if...
You want rate certainty, fewer fees, and a simpler single-approval process from land to move-in.
🔁
Choose Two-Close if...
Your builder isn't lender-approved for one-close programs, or you expect rates to fall and want to shop the permanent loan later.
🏦
Ask Every Lender
Whether their one-close program locks the permanent rate at construction closing, and what the conversion/modification fee is.
