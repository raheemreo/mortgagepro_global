⚠️ Important HO-1 Limitations
Not Available in Many States
Phased out in most states due to inadequate coverage — check availability
Mortgage Lenders May Reject It
Most lenders require at minimum an HO-3 for loan approval
No Theft Coverage
Unlike HO-3/HO-5 — theft of personal property is NOT a named peril in HO-1
Actual Cash Value Only
Pays depreciated value, not full replacement cost — you may receive far less than rebuild cost
Who HO-1 is For
🏚️
Vacant/Seasonal Homes
Low-value or seasonal properties not occupied year-round
🔨
Fixer-Uppers
Renovation projects needing minimal short-term coverage
💰
Very Low Value Homes

🏘️
Investment Properties
Non-owner-occupied structures where landlord policy isn't an option
⚠️ Expert Recommendation
Most insurance professionals do not recommend HO-1 as a long-term policy for primary residences. It covers only 11 named perils, often excludes theft, and pays actual cash value (not replacement cost). If cost is a concern, consider a higher-deductible HO-3 instead — it offers dramatically better protection for a modest premium increase.
