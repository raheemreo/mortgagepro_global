Overseas Investment Act 2005 (as amended 2018 & 2020)
Foreign Buyers Banned in NZ
Since August 2018, most overseas persons are prohibited from purchasing residential land in New Zealand. The ban was a Labour government policy to improve housing affordability for New Zealanders.

📜 Key Legal Milestones
05
Overseas Investment Act 2005
Original framework established · required OIO consent for sensitive land purchases by overseas persons
18
2018 Amendment – Residential Ban
Labour government banned most overseas persons from purchasing residential land · effective 22 October 2018
20
2020 Amendment – Streamlined
Simplified OIO consent process · introduced fast-track for lower-risk investments · new COVID provisions
23
UK FTA 2023 – New Exemptions
NZ-UK Free Trade Agreement granted UK citizens similar rights to Australians for residential property
25
2025 – Act Remains in Force
National-led coalition reviewing OIA · no major changes announced as of Q1 2025 · ban remains

⚠️ Legal Consequences of Non-Compliance
💰
Civil penalties up to $500,000 per breach for individuals. Companies face up to $5 million.
🏠
Forced sale orders – Courts can order disposal of the property, often at a loss.
⚖️
Criminal liability – Deliberate breaches can result in criminal prosecution under the OIA 2005.
📞
Always seek legal advice before purchasing if you are not a NZ citizen or permanent resident. Contact OIO: 04 462 4699.
