Ring-Fencing Timeline
2019 — Law Introduced
Ring-Fencing Rules Begin
Residential rental losses can no longer offset other income (salary, business). Losses are "ring-fenced" to rental portfolio only. Applies to properties acquired before and after.
2021–2024 — Interest Phase-Out
Mortgage Interest Deductibility Phased Out
From Oct 2021: interest deductibility began phasing out for investment properties. By 2024, only 80% was deductible, compounding losses for landlords.
1 Apr 2025 — Interest Restored
Full Interest Deductibility Returns
National-led government restored 100% mortgage interest deductibility from 1 April 2025. Ring-fencing rules remain — losses still cannot offset non-rental income.
2025+ — Current Rules
Ring-Fencing Still Active
Rental losses remain ring-fenced to rental income. Carried-forward losses can be used when property generates profit, or all remaining losses are released at the time of sale.
Key Rules to Know
🔒
What's Ring-Fenced
Losses from residential rental property (houses, apartments, units). Cannot offset salary, self-employment, or business income. Commercial property is NOT ring-fenced.
🔄
Carry Forward Losses
Ring-fenced losses carry forward indefinitely. Use them when rental portfolio turns profitable in future years. All losses released on sale of the property.
🏢
Portfolio vs Individual
You can elect to pool all rental properties together. A profitable property can absorb another's loss within the same portfolio. Useful if you own multiple rentals.
⚠️
Bright-Line Interaction
If you sell a property subject to the bright-line test, accumulated ring-fenced losses from that property are released and can offset the taxable gain on sale.
