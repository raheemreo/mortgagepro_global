What Affects Your Score
Payment History
35% impact
Most important factor. Late or missed payments stay on file for 5 years in NZ. Even one missed payment can drop score 50–100 points.
Credit Utilisation
30% impact
Keep credit card balances below 30% of your limit. Maxed-out cards signal financial stress to lenders.
Length of Credit History
15% impact
Longer history improves your score. Don't close old accounts — keep them open even if unused.
Credit Enquiries
10% impact
Each hard enquiry (loan application) can reduce score by 5–10 points. Multiple enquiries in 14 days are usually grouped as one.
Credit Mix
10% impact
Having a mix of mortgage, car loan and credit card shows you can manage different credit types responsibly.
Improve Your Score — NZ Tips
⏰
Always Pay On Time
Set up automatic payments for minimums. Defaults stay 5 years. Judgments stay 7 years on NZ file.
💳
Reduce Credit Limits
Lenders look at total available credit, not just what's used. Request lower limits before applying for mortgage.
🔍
Check for Errors
Get free Equifax report. Dispute any errors. Correcting mistakes can boost score 50–150 points fast.
🚫
Avoid Buy Now Pay Later
BNPL (Afterpay, Zip) shows as credit commitments in NZ. ANZ and ASB include BNPL in debt assessments.
