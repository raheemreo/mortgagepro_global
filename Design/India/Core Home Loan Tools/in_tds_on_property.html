How to Deposit TDS – Step by Step
1
Deduct TDS at the time of payment
Buyer deducts 1% of the sale consideration (or stamp duty value, whichever is higher) before paying the seller. Applicable only if total consideration exceeds ₹50 Lakh.
Required by Law
2
File Form 26QB online on NSDL
Go to tin.tin.nsdl.com → TDS on Property → Form 26QB. Enter buyer PAN, seller PAN, property details, and TDS amount. One Form 26QB per buyer-seller combination.
Within 30 Days of Payment
3
Pay TDS via challan 26QB
Pay online via Net Banking / debit card through NSDL. Authorised bank challan generated. Keep acknowledgement number. Payment can also be done at authorised bank branches.
Online Net Banking
4
Download Form 16B (TDS Certificate)
After 10–15 days of TDS deposit, download Form 16B from TRACES portal (tdscpc.gov.in). Issue Form 16B to the seller within 15 days of quarterly TDS statement filing.
TRACES Portal
5
Seller claims TDS credit in ITR
Seller views TDS credit in Form 26AS / AIS. Claims TDS refund or reduces tax payable in Income Tax Return for that assessment year. TDS reflects in TRACES within 30 days.
Seller Benefit
Penalties for Non-Compliance
⚠️
Interest for Late TDS Deposit – Sec 201(1A)
1% per month from date TDS was deductible to actual deduction date, PLUS 1.5% per month from deduction to deposit date. Penalty under Sec 271C can be equal to TDS amount.
📋
Prosecution Risk – Sec 276B
If TDS deducted but not deposited, rigorous imprisonment of 3 months to 7 years and fine. File 26QB promptly to avoid notices. IT Department matches 26AS cross-data automatically.
