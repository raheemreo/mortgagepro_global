Complete Deduction Guide
📘
Section 80C – Principal Repayment
Up to ₹1,50,000/year · Investments & Home Loan
›
Annual Deduction Limit
₹1,50,000
Home Loan Principal: Repayment of housing loan principal qualifies. Property must not be sold within 5 years.
Most Used
PPF (Public Provident Fund): Min ₹500, max ₹1.5L/year. 15-year lock-in. Current rate 7.1% p.a.
ELSS Mutual Funds: 3-year lock-in (shortest). Market-linked returns.
Tax + Returns
LIC / Life Insurance Premium: Annual premium up to 10% of sum assured qualifies.
NSC, SCSS, 5-Year FD: Post office schemes and tax-saving bank FDs qualify.
Sukanya Samriddhi: For girl child. Rate 8.2% p.a.
Girl Child Only
Children's Tuition Fees: Full-time education, max 2 children.
💳
Section 24(b) – Interest on Home Loan
Up to ₹2,00,000 (self-occupied) · Unlimited (let-out)
›
Self-Occupied Property Limit
₹2,00,000
Self-Occupied Property: Deduction capped at ₹2,00,000 per annum on interest paid.
Let-Out Property: No upper cap. Full interest amount deductible. Net rental income (after deduction) can be set off.
Pre-Construction Interest: Deductible in 5 equal instalments starting from year of possession.
Joint Home Loan: Each co-borrower can claim up to ₹2,00,000 separately if property is co-owned.
Completion Condition: Property must be acquired/constructed within 5 years from end of FY of loan.
🏠
Section 80EEA – First-Time Buyers
Additional ₹1,50,000 interest · Affordable Housing
›
Additional Interest Deduction
₹1,50,000
First-Time Buyer: You must not own any residential property on the date of loan sanction.
Key Condition
Stamp Duty Value: Property stamp duty value must not exceed ₹45 lakhs.
Loan Sanction Window: Loan sanctioned between 01-Apr-2019 and 31-Mar-2022 (extended). Check current status.
Over and Above 24(b): This is in addition to the ₹2,00,000 deduction under 24(b). Total interest deduction = ₹3.5L.
🏗️
Section 80EE – Additional Interest
₹50,000 extra · Loan ≤ ₹35L · House value ≤ ₹50L
›
Additional Deduction Limit
₹50,000
Loan sanction: Must be sanctioned between 01-Apr-2016 and 31-Mar-2017.
Loan amount: Housing loan must not exceed ₹35 lakhs.
Property value: Residential property value must not exceed ₹50 lakhs.
Note: 80EEA is a superseding benefit for newer loans. Claim only one – 80EE or 80EEA.

Important Conditions
📌 Key Rules to Remember
• 80C limit of ₹1.5L is shared across ALL investments (PPF + ELSS + principal etc.)
• 24(b) deduction requires possession of property within 5 years of loan
• Joint loans allow EACH co-owner to claim deductions separately
• Under New Tax Regime (Default from FY 2023-24), these deductions are NOT available
• Standard Deduction of ₹50,000 is available in both regimes for salaried
